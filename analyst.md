You will be writing research reports on areas within crypto that have been underexplored, driving new ground and exploring new ideas.

For info on our culture at defillama and how we work, read https://github.com/DefiLlama/careers/tree/master#culture.

- Your CV

- A written article on the topic of revenue defensibility for stablecoins

Deadline to apply is **Sunday 5th of July by the end of the day** (UTC+02:00).

For the article, the premise we want to explore is that there seems to be massive differences between stablecoins in how much of a moat they have and how defensible their revenues are.

For example, for stablecoins that are used by other stablecoins as reserves in the backend, as in the case of ethena's USDtb with BUIDL, if another stablecoin offers higher apy, the frontend stablecoin can easily switch to this other stable. Thus their moat is very fickle, they can’t charge much and they’re in danger of seeing a large part of their supply replaced.

On the other hand usdt is held in lots of individual wallets and for every single of those to switch to another stable it would require a massive migration, so this has a much deeper moat.

Another example of this is the usdc/native markets situation on hyperliquid.

Besides holders, you can look at other things that drive a stablecoin moat, such as liquidity or anything else you can think of.

Write an article exploring moats and driving a new perspective that hasn’t been already explored, we want net new research.

Anything that we identify as being AI written will be immediately disqualified from this one and future applications. We are not against AI and you can use it on the job but for applications if AI is allowed we will end up buried in applications.
